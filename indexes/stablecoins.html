Overview

Stablecoins are poised for explosive growth, with an estimated current market around $200B—only a fraction of the potential that could exceed $25T. The key to responsible scaling lies in reducing risk by using permissioned blockchains as the foundational environment, then selectively bridging to public ledgers when beneficial. This approach paves the way for massive adoption while satisfying inevitable regulatory scrutiny.

Core Concepts

1. Permissioned Blockchains

Permissioned chains serve as a “risk-down” environment for scalable stablecoin issuance. They provide a controlled setting to address regulatory requirements and manage systemic risk effectively.

2. Beyond “Money as Collateral”

We tap into the $100T+ value of U.S. equities and real estate. This grants safe yields for investors and low-interest mortgages for homeowners, with complex blockchain layers abstracted away from end users.

3. Inflationary Impact Management

Injecting $25T in liquidity inevitably affects asset prices, especially real estate and equities. Rising valuations help stabilize the system's solvency--but require conservative collateral management to prevent runaway price spirals.

Anticipated Regulations

  • Asset Registration: Stablecoins pegged to other assets will likely need to register similarly to securities, with strict reserve requirements.
  • Supply Caps: Regulators may impose issuance limits based on market conditions and systemic risk assessments.
  • Baseline Interest: Issuers could be required to pay a minimum (1–1.5%) annual interest on outstanding stablecoin value, curbing “upward spiral” risks.
  • Transparency & Audits: Mandatory quarterly disclosures of collateral and liabilities, plus annual third-party audits, are expected to reinforce market confidence.
